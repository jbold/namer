Mapping of phonemes to psychological associations, based on published sound symbolism research (Sapir 1929, Köhler 1929, Ramachandran & Hubbard 2001, Kovic et al. 2010) and cross-cultural naming patterns.

## Phoneme Classes

### Plosives/Stops (b, d, g, k, p, t)
**Effect:** Abrupt, decisive, solid. Creates a sense of firmness and reliability.
**Best for:** Enterprise, infrastructure, security, fintech — anything that needs to feel rock-solid.
**Pattern:** Often strong at word start. "B" especially signals reliability (BlackBerry, Brex, Box).

### Fricatives (f, s, sh, v, z)
**Effect:** Flowing, continuous, alive. Creates motion and energy.
**Best for:** Creative tools, consumer products, anything that should feel dynamic or smooth.
**Pattern:** "V" is the most alive sound in English. "S" creates white noise / smooth sensation. "Z" demands attention.

### Nasals (m, n, ng)
**Effect:** Warm, resonant, approachable. Humming quality.
**Best for:** Community, communication, wellness, platforms that connect people.
**Pattern:** "M" is the most universal comfort sound (mama, mmm). "N" feels natural and near.

### Liquids (l, r)
**Effect:** Flowing, lyrical, luminous (L) or robust, resonant (R).
**Best for:** L = design, creativity, light. R = engineering, power, reliability.
**Pattern:** L-names feel lighter and more elegant. R-names feel more muscular.

### Affricates (ch, j, dg)
**Effect:** Energetic, playful, punchy.
**Best for:** Consumer apps, games, casual/fun brands.

## Vowel Symbolism

### Front Vowels (i as in "bit", e as in "bet")
**Effect:** Small, light, fast, precise, intimate.
**Best for:** Tools, precision instruments, quick actions.
**Examples:** Swiffer (quick action), Figma (precise), Linear (clean).

### Back Vowels (o as in "boat", u as in "boot")
**Effect:** Large, heavy, solid, grounded, powerful.
**Best for:** Infrastructure, platforms, enterprise, gravitas.
**Examples:** Sonos (resonant), Mongo (massive), Roku (solid).

### Open Vowels (a as in "father", ah)
**Effect:** Expansive, open, grand, authoritative.
**Best for:** Platforms, marketplaces, ambitious ventures.
**Examples:** Amazon, Palantir, Kafka.

## Compound Effects

Combining sounds creates emergent effects:

| Combo | Effect | Examples |
|-------|--------|----------|
| V + front vowel | Vibrant + precise = cutting-edge | Vercel, Vimeo |
| S + nasal | Smooth + warm = inviting | Sonos, Sonar |
| Hard stop + liquid | Solid + flowing = powerful grace | Brex, Prisma, Grail |
| Z/X + back vowel | Electric + grounded = futuristic authority | Azure, Zoom, Xero |
| Double consonant | Emphasis, memorability | Ripple, Paddle, Lasso |

## The Bouba/Kiki Principle

Round sounds (m, n, l, o, u, b) → round, soft, friendly, approachable brands.
Sharp sounds (k, t, p, i, e, x, z) → angular, precise, technical, innovative brands.

Most successful names blend both — enough roundness for approachability, enough sharpness for distinctiveness. Pure round = forgettable. Pure sharp = hostile.
